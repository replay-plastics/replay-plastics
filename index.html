The challenge

Recycled plastic loses what makes it useful.

Each time a plastic is recycled, its polymer chains degrade. Mechanical properties — tensile strength, impact resistance, flexibility — deteriorate with every cycle.

This forces manufacturers to blend recycled material with virgin plastic, limiting the real circularity of the value chain and keeping the industry dependent on fossil feedstocks.

The result: a recycling sector that processes more volume but delivers lower-quality material, narrowing the market for truly circular products.

Our technology

A drop-in additive that restores mechanical integrity.

Replay develops a reactive additive — our recycling agent — that reconstructs degraded polymer chains during the melt-processing stage. No new equipment. No process disruption. Just better material out.

♻️

Degraded feedstock in

Post-consumer or post-industrial recycled plastic, regardless of degradation level, enters a standard extrusion process.

⚗️

Replay agent added

Our additive is dosed directly at the extruder. It reacts with broken chain ends, rebuilding molecular weight and restoring structure.

✅

Virgin-grade performance out

The output material meets or exceeds the mechanical specs of virgin equivalents — ready for demanding applications.
